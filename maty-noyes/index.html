The Story
Mississippi Hippie
With A Dream

Madeline Ashley "Maty" Noyes grew up in Corinth, Mississippi — a small town where she always felt like an outsider. She got her first guitar at 12, wrote her first song within a week, and knew music was her escape.

The day she turned 16, she moved to Nashville alone. Her parents gave her a list of conditions they thought she'd fail. She cleared every single one. By 17, she was in Los Angeles with a publishing deal.

At 18, she sang background vocals on The Weeknd's "Angel" and co-wrote and voiced Kygo's "Stay" — a tropical house anthem that hit #50 on the Billboard Hot 100 and earned Gold in 6 countries.

In 2016, a public conflict with Kygo's manager threatened to derail her career. Kesha publicly backed her on Instagram. It defined her: she wouldn't be silenced by the industry.

She left Republic Records, released her debut album in 2021, and has been fully independent since 2022 — a self-described "psychedelic pop artist."
